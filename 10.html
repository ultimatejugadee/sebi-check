The Taj Mahal: A Symbol of Eternal Love

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

It is regarded by many as the best example of Mughal architecture and a symbol of India's rich history. The Taj Mahal attracts 7-8 million visitors a year and in 2007, it was declared a winner of the New7Wonders of the World initiative.

Diverse Architectural Styles

Indian architecture encompasses a multitude of expressions over space and time, constantly absorbing new ideas. The result is an evolving range of architectural production that nonetheless retains a certain amount of continuity across history.

From the rock-cut caves of Ajanta and Ellora to the colonial buildings of Mumbai, from the stepwells of Gujarat to the temples of Tamil Nadu, Indian architecture is as diverse as its culture.
